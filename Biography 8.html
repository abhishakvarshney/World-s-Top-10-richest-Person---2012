Biography-:

Stefan Persson (born 4 October 1947) is a Swedish business magnate. He is the current chairman and main shareholder in fashion company H&M, which was founded by his father Erling Persson. Stefan took over the company in 1982 and served as its chief executive officer until 1998. According to Forbes magazine, Persson had a net worth US$26 billion in March 2012, making him the richest person in Sweden and the 8th richest person in the world.

Stefan also owns a substantial stake in Swedish technology company Hexagon, and privately held properties in London, Paris and Stockholm.

He is a supporter of Djurgårdens IF and is co-funding a foundation for the club.[citation needed] He also owns the small English village of Linkenholt.

Persson attended the University of Stockholm. He lives in Stockholm, Sweden and is married to Carolyn Denise Persson. They have three children. In 2009 his son, Karl-Johan Persson, took over as president and chief executive of H&M.
